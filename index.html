01

Opening Range Breakout (ORB)

High Frequency

Primary users: stolpilot, carlo, L U C A S, Liam (リアム), Sosa in this beamer — the most commonly executed model in the chat

Step-by-Step Approach

  1. 1
    Mark the first 5-minute candle's high and low at the 9:30 AM NY open as your ORB range.
  2. 2
    Mark the midpoint (50%) of the ORB — this level is treated as a magnet and key decision zone. Members call it "ORB CE" or "midpoint."
  3. 3
    Wait for price to respect or sweep one extreme of the ORB (high or low), then look for rejection back into the range.
  4. 4
    On a retest of the ORB midpoint or low/high, look for a confirming 1m or 5m FVG forming at that level.
  5. 5
    Enter on the break + close of the confirming candle. Stop goes just beyond the ORB extreme. Target is the opposite end of the ORB, then beyond.
  6. 6
    If price inverses the FVG that was used as the entry signal (IFVG), this becomes a new bearish/bullish bias point — consider re-entry in the opposite direction.
stolpilot executes exclusively on the 1m, market orders only. He draws the ORB on the 5m and 15m — never smaller. "5 min is too small TF" — his 15m ORB is his trusted zone.
02

Optimal Trade Entry (OTE)

Intermediate

Primary users: stolpilot, ZMG, BaNQo, MarkTooSharp — described as a "continuation model, not a reversal"

Step-by-Step Approach

  1. 1
    Identify the most recent significant swing high and swing low on the 15m or 1H chart.
  2. 2
    Draw a Fibonacci retracement from the swing low to swing high (for bullish) or swing high to swing low (for bearish). Include wicks.
  3. 3
    Wait for price to take external liquidity (sweep the most recent swing point) and then begin a pull-back.
  4. 4
    Watch for price to enter the OTE zone: between the 0.62–0.79 Fibonacci levels. The 0.70 level is the "sweet spot" — stolpilot watches for price to reject at 0.70 specifically.
  5. 5
    Look for a confirming PD array inside the OTE zone: a bearish/bullish FVG, Order Block, or Breaker Block.
  6. 6
    Enter at the PDA inside OTE. Stop goes just beyond the 1.0 Fibonacci level (the swing). Target is the opposite liquidity pool or the next significant DOL.
stolpilot: "OTE is a continuation model not a reversal." BaNQo took an OTE pre-market — "OTE was gonna hold up and reverse and the volatility was there." Confirmed by stolpilot: "yes. PDL taken pre market. Pull back into OTE between 8:30 and 11, OTE level aligned with a PDA then wait for a confirmation of a reversal."
03

Liquidity Sweep + Market Structure Shift

Core Model

Primary users: OL2G, Tommy Shelby, ZMG, 1suf, Doozi — the foundational entry model most members use as a baseline

Step-by-Step Approach

  1. 1
    Identify your HTF bias (daily/4H/1H) — are we in a premium or discount? Mark the directional draw on liquidity (DOL).
  2. 2
    On the 15m or 5m, identify resting liquidity above (buy-side: equal highs, swing highs, BSL) or below (sell-side: equal lows, swing lows, SSL).
  3. 3
    Wait for price to aggressively sweep through that liquidity — a strong spike that takes the level and immediately reverses.
  4. 4
    After the sweep, watch for a Market Structure Shift (MSS): a displacement candle that closes beyond the most recent internal swing point in the opposite direction.
  5. 5
    Wait for a pullback into the FVG created by the displacement candle (the "CISD" — change in state of delivery — FVG).
  6. 6
    Enter at the 50% or OB level of the CISD FVG. Stop above/below the sweep candle's wick. Target the next external liquidity pool in the direction of the MSS.
OL2G's rule: "Before you look for entry it needs to take some sort of liquidity. Did it take liquidity?" — This is non-negotiable. No sweep = no trade.
04

Silver Bullet / Killzone Model

Time-Based

Primary users: DannyDayTrades, Porgy, ZMG, ray, Poizo — uses ICT's specific killzone windows

Step-by-Step Approach

  1. 1
    Mark your Silver Bullet windows: 10:00–11:00 AM NY time (primary) and 2:00–3:00 PM NY time (secondary).
  2. 2
    Determine HTF bias before the window opens. Do NOT wait for the window to figure out bias — the window is only for entry.
  3. 3
    As the window opens (10:00 AM candle), wait for price to manipulate — sweep of a short-term high or low within the window.
  4. 4
    Look for a 1m SIBI (sell-side imbalance buy-side inefficiency) or BISI forming after the sweep — this is the "Silver Bullet setup."
  5. 5
    Enter at the SIBI/BISI. Stop goes above/below the manipulation wick. Target: previous liquidity pool from overnight, Asia, or London session.
  6. 6
    If the Silver Bullet fails within 15–20 minutes, re-evaluate. Members close out and wait for the 2 PM window instead.
Porgy: "I believe this is silver bullet, I started entering in this 1m SIBI that was overlapping with NWOG low." — The SIBI overlapping a key level (NWOG, PDL, PDH) is the A+++ version of this setup.
05

PO3 / AMD (Accumulation, Manipulation, Distribution)

Macro Model

Primary users: Wixx_♧, OL2G, ZMG, Sosa in this beamer — used to understand the narrative of the entire day or session

Step-by-Step Approach

  1. 1
    Identify the dealing range: the swing high and swing low for the current session or daily candle. Mark the CE (50%).
  2. 2
    Look for the Accumulation phase: price consolidating in a narrow range, building orders. This is NOT a V-shape — it's a flat-ish consolidation.
  3. 3
    The Manipulation phase: price breaks in the WRONG direction — takes out old liquidity on one side (stop run). This is the "fake" move. Wixx: OL2G taught "accumulation is before ext liq is taken."
  4. 4
    Enter AFTER the manipulation is confirmed — a strong reversal candle back through the range with an MSS or displacement.
  5. 5
    The Distribution phase is the "real" move in the opposite direction of the manipulation. Target the external liquidity on the other side.
  6. 6
    Sessions can also act as AMD — Asia = Accumulation, London = Manipulation, NY = Distribution. OL2G: "as they also can set up AMD: Asia, London, NY."
OL2G: "Consolidation and accumulation are very different models." Consolidation takes internal liquidity; accumulation takes external liquidity with the manipulation move — don't confuse them.
06

HTF PD Array + Seconds-TF Entry

High RR / Low WR

Primary user: OL2G — 30–40% win rate, 20–65 RR trades. "POI, TS, COS FVG, IR" — his model in shorthand

Step-by-Step Approach

  1. 1
    Every weekend: mark every HTF PD Array (FVGs, OBs, Rejection Blocks) on the 15m, 1H, 4H, Daily, and Weekly charts. Map all external liquidity pools.
  2. 2
    Identify the highest-quality HTF POI: a PD Array that has (a) previous reactions at that level, (b) external liquidity nearby, and (c) falls in an OTE zone.
  3. 3
    Wait for price to reach that HTF POI. The POI must tap — do not enter early.
  4. 4
    On a seconds-level chart (30s or tick chart), watch for a Change of State of Delivery (COS) — a displacement candle + FVG in the opposite direction at the HTF POI.
  5. 5
    Enter market order inside the COS FVG. Stop goes just above/below the most recent swing. Targets are the next HTF liquidity pool.
  6. 6
    Hold the trade hours if needed — OL2G held a trade from open (9:30 AM) for 7 hours, targeting HOD to LOD for 321 points at 65RR.
OL2G: "15m PD Arrays are like gold if they have external liq close to them." His rule: a HTF PD Array with external liquidity AND previous reactions = highest probability POI. He uses market orders on a seconds chart because "the fvg may be a bag" on the minutes TF.
07

ICT + Orderflow / Footprint Hybrid

Advanced

Primary users: Cyph3r, Boy Wonder, carlo — combines ICT zones with footprint/bookmap confirmation for precision entries

Step-by-Step Approach

  1. 1
    Mark your chart normally with ICT PD Arrays (FVGs, OBs, Breakers). These define the "where."
  2. 2
    Load a footprint chart (ATAS, Sierra Charts, or Tradovate's footprint) or Bookmap alongside your ICT chart.
  3. 3
    When price approaches an ICT POI, zoom into the footprint. Look for: absorption (large sell volume at lows not pushing lower / large buy volume at highs not pushing higher), stacked resting limit orders (visible on Bookmap as a "wall"), or delta exhaustion.
  4. 4
    The footprint tells you WHICH FVG or OB within the ICT zone will actually hold — because it shows where real orders are sitting.
  5. 5
    Enter only when footprint confirms ICT level. Stop remains ICT-based (beyond the PDA). Target ICT DOL.
  6. 6
    Also use GEX (gamma exposure) from gexbot.com — market maker options hedging creates magnets at specific levels. Cyph3r: "es 6400, nq 23250 liquidity" — these are GEX-derived magnets.
Cyph3r: "ICT attempts to predict where the orderflow will be, those tools show where it actually is. So they work well together." Carlo: "footprint is better as that can't be faked" — unlike spoofed limit orders on bookmap.

// 03 — Bias & Direction

HOW TO IDENTIFY DAILY BIAS

From NJ: "I must have seen tons of videos on finding Daily Bias. Never found clarity." — Here's how the experienced members actually do it, synthesized from the entire chat.

Step 1 — HTF Context (Weekly / Daily)

  • Is the market in a premium or discount zone? (Above or below 50% of the weekly range)
  • Where are the weekly FVGs, OBs, and Breakers that price is approaching?
  • What's the directional draw — are there unfilled imbalances or liquidity pools above or below?
  • Is there a protected high/low (has one side NOT been swept yet this week)?
  • Daily: Did price take the PDH or PDL pre-market? This tells you if London manipulated up or down.

Step 2 — Session Structure (4H / 1H)

  • Check: Did Asia create a clear high or low? Mark Asia High (AH) and Asia Low (AL).
  • London: Did London take the Asia High or Low? This is the manipulation. NY should do the opposite.
  • Mark London High and London Low — at NY open, whichever was NOT swept is the target.
  • Look at 4H structure: Is the last 4H candle bullish or bearish? Is there a 4H FVG above or below?
  • ZMG's rule: "Sometimes you won't really have a clear daily bias. Those days you either sit out or employ 'if price does this, I do this.'"

Step 3 — Intraday SMT Divergence

  • Compare NQ vs ES (or Gold vs Silver for XAUUSD traders). Do they make the SAME swing point, or does one fail?
  • If NQ makes a new low but ES does NOT → SMT divergence → bullish bias. Vice versa for bearish.
  • SMT is most powerful at session open (8:30–9:30) and again at 10AM.
  • Robin: "We got a big ass SMT" was his entry trigger for the day's directional trade.
  • OL2G: "SMTs a bonus" — don't rely on it alone; it confirms but doesn't replace HTF context.

Step 4 — Confirming at Open

  • Watch the 9:30 open candle — does it gap up or down? Mark the NWOG (New Week Opening Gap) and check if price runs to fill it.
  • At 9:30: does price immediately reject a PD Array or does it blow through? Rejection = bias confirmed.
  • carlo: "I think we go down initially and then up" — this is AMD awareness at open.
  • NJ: "I wait for MSS towards down and then go" — waits for displacement in the bias direction before entering.
  • Meowmeow: "I look at higher TF from Daily to about 1HR. Sometimes weekly, especially now with gold breaking past weekly levels."

Bias Filters — When to Stay Out

❌ Skip the Day When:
  • — FOMC day (unpredictable expansion in either direction)
  • — Choppy, "barcode" price action with no clear range
  • — You've already hit your daily loss limit
  • — Price is between PD Arrays with no clear POI nearby
  • — High-resistance liquidity runs — "messy" price action
✓ High-Confidence Bias Day When:
  • — London clearly swept Asia high/low with displacement
  • — SMT divergence confirmed at session open
  • — Price in a HTF premium/discount with clear DOL
  • — TGIF Friday: previous week low already swept, longs set up
  • — MMBM confirmed (Monday's range established)
// 04 — Elite Setups

THE A+++ TRADE CHECKLIST

From ZMG and Poizo's "Unicorn" discussion to OL2G's 65RR trade — here's exactly what makes a setup go from "okay" to "once a week, screenshot-worthy."

ALL OF THESE MUST ALIGN — NO EXCEPTIONS

✓
HTF Confluence (15m or above) A PD Array on the 15m, 1H, or 4H chart at the entry point. Not a random level — a proven area where price has reacted before.
✓
External Liquidity Nearby A pool of buy-stops or sell-stops resting just above/below the POI that has NOT been taken yet. This gives price a reason to reach your level.
✓
Clear DOL (Draw on Liquidity) You can clearly see WHERE price wants to go after the entry. A specific pool of liquidity (equal highs, old swing, weekly level) that provides the target.
✓
Session Alignment + Time Window The setup appears at 9:30–11AM, 2PM–3PM, or during a London KZ (2–5AM). Setups outside of killzones are lower probability. NJ: "I use 10AM 4H candle along with AMD."
✓
Overlapping PD Arrays Two or more PD arrays stacking at the same price: FVG + OB, Breaker + FVG ("Unicorn"), NWOG + 15m FVG, OTE zone + FVG. Poizo: "A Breaker paired with a FVG is considered a Unicorn model."
✓
SMT Divergence at Entry At the exact POI, NQ and ES (or Gold and Silver) diverge — one makes a new extreme and the other doesn't. This is the market maker's fingerprint. Not required, but makes it A+++.
✓
Previous Reaction at the Level Price has ALREADY reacted at this exact level before, bouncing cleanly. OL2G's rule: "if a POI aka HTF PD Array has EXL and previous reactions that respect the base or CE, it becomes a very high POI for reversals."
✓
Minimum 2:1 RR (ideally 3:1+) The distance to your DOL must be at least 2× your stop loss distance. ray: "If the RR is less than 2 I won't even enter the trade, the reward isn't worth the risk."

Bonus Confirmations That Elevate to A+++

→ Footprint confirmation: Absorption visible at the POI on the footprint chart (large volume with no price movement)
→ GEX magnet alignment: GEX levels from options market makers at or near your target
→ 9/21 EMA confluence: "When 15m FVGs line up with the 9 and/or 21 MA they go CRAZY" — Liam
→ Volume Profile POC: Entry at or near the Point of Control (POC) from the previous session's volume profile
→ TGIF model: Friday setup — previous week's low is swept, all Friday's early lows are swept, OTE is aligned = highest probability long of the week
